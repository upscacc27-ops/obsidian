### Evolution
[[Art 40  organization of village panchayats]] under DPSPs acknowledges the importance of Panchayats, although they gained constitutional recognition, but the structured differed across states, because the directions were not legally binding, several committees were established to promote uniformity
- **Balwant Rai Mehta Committee 1957** - recommended 3-tier model - 
	- Zila Parishad
	- Panchayat Samiti
	- Gram Panchayat
	- Rajasthan & Andhra were the first to adopt the system
- **Ashok Mehta Committee 1978** - proposed 2 tier system - taxation powers for Panchayats, constitutional recognition
- **GVK Rao Committee 1985** - emphasized Zilla Prashid's role in developmental & suggested appointment of a District Development Commissioner
- **LM Singhvi 1986** - recommendation constitutional recognition for PRIs, establishment of Nyay Panchayats
- [[73rd CAA 1992]] - gave constitutional status to PRIs - Part IX, provisions from Art 243 to Art 243(O), new 11th Schedule with 29 subjects

### Important Constitutional provisions
- **Art 243B**- Direct election of members of Panchayats at village, intermediate & district levels. specifies tenure, & process of dissolution of PRIs
- **Art 243D** - provides for reservation of Seats for **SCs** & **STs** in PRIs, proportionately to population. Also reserves 1/3rd for seats for women
- **Art 243G** - empowers State legislature to provide Panchayats with the authority & powers to function as self-government institutions
- **Art 243H** - State legislatures are empowered to authorize Panchayats to levy, collect & appropriate taxes, duties
- **Art 243I** - mandates formation of State Finance Commissions every 5 years by governor
- **Art 243K** - provides for establishment of independent State Election Commissioner

### Reforms
- [[2nd ARC]]- 
	- Strengthen Gram Sabha- not only supervise but directly involve in planning economic development & social justice programs
	-  devolution of functions, funds, functionaries - transfer all responsibilities listed in 11th schedule to PRIs
	- Capacity building & training - establish special institutions for training & research 
	- State Finance Commission - recommend sufficient transfer of resources from state to local bodies
	- Separate Election Commission for Local bodies
	- E-governance & Citizen Participation - leverage information & communication technologies for e-governance 
- build local taxation capacity - digitize property records, granting panchayats more freedom
- SHG-Panchayat Linkages - SHGs empower marginalized women to participate in local elections
- Improve data quality - enhance accessibility by using visual maps, interactive charts that can be easily understood by citizens
- Develop scoring system, competition & accountability

### Conclusion
- PRIs in India play a crucial role in strengthening grassroot democracy by promoting local self-governance & participatory development. 
- They empower rural communities ensuring inclusive decision making & effective implementation of development programs at  village level
