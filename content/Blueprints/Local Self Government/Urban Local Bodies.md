### Constitutional mandate
- [[74th CAA 1992]] forms the basis of ULBs in India, it added Part IX-A, consisting of Art 243-P to Art 243-ZG, added 12th schedule

### Type of ULBs
- **Municipal Corporations** - population > 1 million. Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai, Kolkata
- **Municipality - Municipal Committees** - < 1 million people, smaller cities
- **Notified Area Committee** - fast developing towns & towns lacking basic amenities. All the members are nominated by state government
- **Town Area Committee** - small towns. minimal authority such as street lighting, drainage
- **Cantonment Board** - civilians settled in cantonment area, created & run by central govt.
- **Township** - staff & workers living in the colonies established near the plant. no elected members & is merely an extension of bureaucratic structure
- **Port Trust** - near port areas - Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata. maintains ports.
- **Special Purpose agency** - undertake designated activities or specific functions belonging to municipal corporations & municipalities

### Significance
- Urban Planning & Development - land-use planning, infra development, implementing policies
- Service delivery - water supply, sanitation, solid waste management, street lighting etc
- Disaster & Pandemic management - Mumbai floods
- Empowerment of women & marginalized 
- Community participation - reflective of Gandhiji's dream of Poorna Swaraj through democratic decentralization

### Challenges
- Funding
	- Dependence on Centre & state transfers - Acc to RBI report in 2022 on Municipal Finances - ULBs are dependent on state transfers for 35% of their revenue
	- Reduced revenue raising options post GST - subsumption of major sources of revenue - sales tax, local entertainment taxes, increase in Cess & surcharges
	- incapacity of raising direct finances from the assigned taxes - ES 2018 pointed out failure of ULBs in realizing full value of property taxes, which is exclusive domain of ULBs
	- Narrow taxation powers - as compared to China, Denmark, US
	- Lack of timely constitution of SFCs 
- Functional 
	- Parastatal agencies - created by state govt deny autonomy
	- improper devolution - inadequate
	- Lack of Constitution of District Planning Committees - as provided in 74th CAA, for consolidation & integration of development plans prepared by PRIs & ULBs. - Non functional in 9 states, failed to prepare plans in 15 states
- Functionaries
	- delays in elections - BBMP  - Bangalore Corporation - no elections since late 2020s
	- Criminalization - large number of criminals/corrupt backgrounds
	- Corporator Pati
	- Bureaucratic Control - Municipal Commissioners appointed by state govts. with Mayor just being a ceremonial head
	- Lack of Staff - large number of vacancies
- Functionality
	- failure to plan holistic urban development - slums, traffic congestion, poorly planned settlements
	- Corruption scandals, only 11/35 of States/UTs have enacted public disclosure law that mandates publishing of key civic data
	- Poor coordination

### Way forward
- 6th ARC - 
	- constitution of metropolitan planning committee - prepare developmental plans, review plans of various local bodies
	- establishment of Unified Metropolitan Transport Authority - improve public transportation & traffic congestion
	- Strengthening ULBs - regular elections, devolve maximum functions, adequate resources
	- Creation of National Urban development & housing fund - 
	- implementation of PPP model, e-governance - citizen grievance redressal mechanism
- NITI Ayog
	- improve financial management - adequate resources to raise their own revenues, timely constitution of SFCs
	- capacity building - 
	- strengthening citizen participation - Ward committees, NGOs etc.
- CAG's recommendations
	- emphasis on enhancing ULB autonomy & participation in key areas like urban planning. Stronger laws, policies & institutional frameworks

### Conclusion
ULBs form the backbone of urban governance, yet chronic financial, functional & institutional weaknesses continue to impede their effectiveness.
Strengthening autonomy, capacity & accountability of ULBs in India is essential for building resilient, well governed & sustainable cities
