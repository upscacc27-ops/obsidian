> [!Note] Justice Abhay Manohar
> In my considered opinion, the right to privacy of any individual is essentially a natural right, which inheres in every human being by birth. Such right remains with the human being till he/she breathes last.

### Meaning & Concept
- Right to Privacy is natural & human right enjoyed by every individual by virtue of their existence
- Includes right to be left alone & exercise control over personal information, body, choices & life decisions without unwarranted interference

### Constitutional Provisions
- Not mentioned explicitly in the Constitution - judicially recognized as FR
- derived primarily from Art 21, Art 14, Art 19
### Legal & Statutory Provisions
- IT Act,  2000 - Section 43A protects sensitive personal data
- Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 - protects personal data, regulate date collection & processing, prevents misuse
### Landmark judgement
[[2017 Justice KS Puttaswamy v UOI]]

### Dimensions
- Bodily Privacy - protects Physical integrity, protect intrusion into body without consent - forced medical test & biometric collection
- Information Privacy - Control over collection, use, sharing of personal info - especially digital
- Decisional Autonomy - personal choices - marriage, reproduction, sexual orientation, lifestyle
- Spatial Privacy - No arbitrary surveillance, search intrusion into personal space
- Privacy of Communication - No unlawful interception of phone calls, emails etc.

### Conclusion
Right to privacy, affirmed under [[2017 Justice KS Puttaswamy v UOI]] is a cornerstone of dignity & personal liberty.
Its effective protection requires careful balance between individual liberty & legitimate state interests of security & governance
