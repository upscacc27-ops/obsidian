### Context
Karnataka became the first state in India to enact standalone legislation specifically targeting hate speech & hate motivated conduct *Karnataka Hate Speech & Hate Crimes (Prevention) Bill 2025*
Passed by State legislature, pending presidential assent

### Introduction
In report of Law Commission of India, 2017, defined it as incitement to hatred primarily against a group of persons defined in terms of race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, religious beliefs etc

### Regulatory frameworks
- **Constitutional Frameworks** 
	- intersects with [[Art 19(1)(a) Freedom of Speech or Expression]]
	- Art 19(2) allows state to put reasonable restrictions in interest of sovereignty, integrity, security, friendly relations with foreign states, public order, decency, morality, contempt of court, defamation or incitement of offence
- **IPC**
	- Section 153A, 153B - punish acts of hatred, enmity, ill will between different groups
	- Section 295A - penalizes deliberate acts outraging religious feelings
	- Section 505(1) & 505(2) - publishing or circulating content causing hostility or hatred is an offence
- **Representation of People's Act, 1951**
	- Section 8: Convicts of abusive speech cannot contest elections - disqualified
	- Section 123(3A) & 125 - Classify promotion of hatred for electoral gains as corruption
- **Others**
	- SC & ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 
	- Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955 - penalizes representations that incite untouchability or discrimination

Hate speech undermines social harmony & democratic values, yet freedom of expression must be preserved. 
must adopt a multi-dimensional approach - strengthening laws, promoting counter speech, leveraging technology and engaging communities.
