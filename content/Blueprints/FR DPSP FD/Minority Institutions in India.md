### Context
7 judge bench of SC, in 4:3 ruling, established a "holistic & realistic" test to assess the "minority status" of an educational institution.

### Background of this Case
- **S Azeez Basha v UOI 1967** - SC ruled that AMU did not qualify as a minority institution under Art 30, as it was neither established nor administered by the Muslim minority, AMU's creation through AMU Act of 1920, prevented its qualification as a minority institution
- **Amendment of the AMU Act, 1981** - GOI amended the act, recognizing that it was established by the Muslim Community for their cultural & educational advancement
- **Allahabad HC striking down Muslim reservation, 2006** - In 2005, AMU introduced 50% reservation for Muslims in post-graduate medical courses, however Allahabad HC struck down both the reservation & 1981 amendment. referred to 1967 ruling.

### Recent ruling
SC defined following key elements to determine the minority character of an institution
1. Purpose - preserve minority language & culture, does not need to be the sole purpose
2. Admission - admitting non-minority students does not compromise the character
3. Secular Education- may provide secular education without losing status
4. Religious instruction - Aided institutions cannot make it compulsory, full funded cannot offer. However they will retain their status

### SC's New Test for Minority Character
1. **Establishment** - institution's origin & purpose of founding, who financed the institution, how land was acquired, who oversaw its development
2. **Administration** - Minority institutions have the choice, not the obligation to appoint administrators from own community. However if the administration does not align with minority's interests, implies that it is not primarily for minority's benefit

### Constitutional protection
- [[Art 30 Rights of minorities to establish & administer educational institutions]]
- [[Art 15 Prohibition to Discrimination]] - exempts MEIs from reservations for SEBCs

### Conclusion
SC's latest judgement has outlined a nuanced approach to assess the minority character of the institutions. This is based on institution's founding intent & administrative structure.
