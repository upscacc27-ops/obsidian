### Constitutional Provisions
103rd CAA provided for reservation to EWS
- [[Art 15 Prohibition to Discrimination]] - 15(6) enables up to 10% EWS reservation in educational institutions
- [[Art 16 Equality of Opportunity in Public Employment]] - Art 16(6) 10% reservation in initial appointments in public jobs

### Evolution

| Title                           | Development                                                                                                 |
| ------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| PV Narsimha Rao govt's decision | provided 10% EWS reservation for govt posts and services                                                    |
| Mandal Case                     | SC struck down above provision - Constitution does not provide for poverty basis as grounds for reservation |
| [[Sinho Commission 2010]]       | reservations to EWS granted                                                                                 |
| 103rd CAA 2019                  | inserted Art 15(6), 16(6)                                                                                   |

### Need & Challenges


| Needs                                                          | Challenges                                                                                      |
| -------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Inequality and exclusion due to financial capacity             | Ambiguous definition of theshold                                                                |
| remove stigma associated with reservation                      | exclusion of SC/ST/OBC poor                                                                     |
| recognition of economic backwardness (outside SEBCs)           | breach of 50% cap - breach of merit                                                             |
| inclusive welfare beyond caste - broader principle of equality | implementation & verification challenges - false income certificates - ineligible beneficiaries |

### Way forward

- Rationalize eligibility criteria - improved targeting
- Robust verification mechanism - income  + assets by IT returns, property records
- Periodic review and data driven policy
- reduced struggle to get classified 'backward'

### Conclusion
future of EWS Reservation lies in making it more targeted, transparent and complementary
should strengthen India's broader commitment to equality without undermining social justice mechanisms
