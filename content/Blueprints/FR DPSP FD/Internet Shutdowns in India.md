>[!Note] Supreme Court in [[2020 Anuradha Bhasin v UOI]]
>Freedom of Speech & Expression through the medium of internet enjoys constitutional protection under [[Art 19(1)(a) Freedom of Speech or Expression]]

### Introduction
refers to intentional disruptions to internet or electronic communications, rendering them effectively unusable for a specific location
Typically justified on grounds of  public emergency, safety, national security and public order

### Constitutional Provisions
- CrPC - Upto 2017, used under Section 144 of erstwhile CrPC (Section 163 if BNS)
- Telecommunications Act, 2023 - temporary suspension
- Art 19(2) - Allows govt to impose reasonable restrictions on freedom of speech & expression

### Arguments

### Way forward
- **Parliamentary Standing Committee on Communications & IT**
	- Adopt global best practices - study and adopt
	- codify grounds objectively - parameters to decide the merit/necessity
	- principles of proportionality - Clear codification & procedure for lifting - no indefinite
	- selective banning- minimum inconvenience & maximum curbing of misinformation
	- study effectiveness by DoT & MHA

### Conclusion
- sometimes justified on grounds of maintaining public order, impose significant economic, social and freedom-based costs. Balanced, proportionate & transparent grounds is essential to safeguard national security & constitutional freedoms
