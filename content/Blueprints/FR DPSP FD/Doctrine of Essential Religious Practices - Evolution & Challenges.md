>The doctrine of essentiality is a doctrine that has evolved to protect the religious practices that are essential and integral and does not violate any fundamental right

### Introduction
Essential religious practice test is a doctrine evolved by the SC to protect only such religious practices under FRs, which are essential & integral to religion
invented by 7-judge Bench of SC in **Shir Mutt Case in 1954**

Indian Constitution guarantees freedom of religion under 
- [[Art 25 Freedom of conscience & free profession, practice &   propagation of religion]], 
- [[Art 26 Freedom to manage religious affairs]],
- [[Art 27 Freedom as to payments of taxes for promotion of religion]]
- [[Art 28 Freedom as to attendance of religious instruction in educational institutions]]
Indian mode of Secularism is not negative - provides for state's intervention to ensure freedom of religion

### Examples of use of ERP Doctrine
- 1958 Sri Venkataramana Devaru v State of Mysore - SC had to decide whether restrictions on entry to temples for some sections was an **essential** part of the Hindu religion - No, held that such practices are unconstitutional & opened temples for all Hindus
- 1994 Ismail Faruqui Case - In Ayodhya Case, court held that offering prayers is an essential part of Islam, offering prayers in a mosque is not
- Ananda Marg Sect - 2004, SC held that Ananda Marg Sect had no fundamental right to perform Tandav dance in streets, not essential practice
- [[2018 Indian Young Lawyers Association v State of Kerala]]

### Conclusion
Doctrine seeks to protect religious practices by safeguarding core beliefs, but put courts in difficult position to decide the essentiality, while it balances faith & constitutional values, subjective application and potential of judicial overreach continue to raise concerns around secularism
