### Introduction
- Scope of Section 66A, IT Act, 2000 - messages sent via communication services, including emails, SMS, social media
- Offences covered - criminalized sending 
	- grossly offensive or menacing information, 
	- false or misleading information causing annoyance, inconvenience, danger, obstruction, insult or injury
	- info intended to disrupt public order
- Punishment up to 3 years imprisonment
- Sender must have intent to cause harm, insult, annoyance or danger

### Conclusion
Section 66A was struck down as its vague language violated [[Art 19(1)(a) Freedom of Speech or Expression]] and posed a threat to free speech
While online hate & harmful content needs regulation, laws must be precise, reasonable & proportionate
Balanced framework combining clear legal definitions, intermediary accountability and civic awareness can prevent misuse.
focus should remain on protecting society without stifling legitimate expression
