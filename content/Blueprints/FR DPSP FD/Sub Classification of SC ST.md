
### Context
7 judge bench of the SC headed by CJI, ruled 6:1, allowed for sub classification of SC/ST
This decision overturns 2004 ruling EV Chinnaiah v State of Andhra P. which had prohibited sub-classification

### Latest Judgement Pointers
[[2024 Davinder Singh v State of Punjab]]
- Permission for sub-classification - States can sub-classify for better targeting - sub division of existing 15% SC quota
- Sub Classification basis - must be based on historical evidence and quantifiable data - rather than politically motivated & arbitrary
- Creamy Layer in SC/ST - exclude affluent individuals
- Subject to Judicial Review - to prevent misuse for political gain
- Generational limit - a family member already availed benefit and achieved higher social status - subsequent generations may not qualify

### Way forward
- Consensus Building - Political parties, community leaders essential
- Effective monitoring - prevent leakage and gaming
- Maintaining social harmony 
- Strengthening the administrative and institutional capacity of states - training personnel, data management systems, dedicated agencies
- Adequate funding to implement & monitor

### Conclusion
- crucial for policymakers to engage in comprehensive dialogues with all stakeholders - community representatives, legal experts & social scientists.
- May constitute a commission on the lines of **G Rohini Commission** for OBC sub-classification
- Aim to address disparities within SC category while preserving unity & collective progress of the community as a whole
