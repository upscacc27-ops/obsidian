### Evolution of Education as an FR

|                      |                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Original provision   | [[Art 45 DPSP Early Childhood Care & Education]]                                                                                                                                       |
| Judicial Proceedings | 1992 Mohini Jain v State of Karnataka - SC held that Right to Education is a part of [[Art 21 Right to life and personal liberty]]<br><br>[[1993 Unni Krishnan v State of Andhra]]<br> |
| [[86th CAA 2002]]    | inserted [[Art 21A Right to Education]]                                                                                                                                                |
### Evolution of the act
- enacted 2009, provides 25% reservation in private institutions
- Society for unaided private schools of Rajasthan v UOI - private schools challenged the 25% reservation but SC upheld the validity, but exempted unaided minority institutions from reservations

### Key Provisions
- incarnation of [[Art 21A Right to Education]] 
- provides that every child 6-14 years of age has the right to free & compulsory elementary education
- clarifies **compulsory education** means **obligation of appropriate govt** to provide free elementary education - admission + attendance + completion, no liability for payments
- non-admitted child to be admitted to an age-appropriate class
- duty of appropriate govts, local authorities, parents in providing education - financial responsibilities shared between states & central govt
- norms & standards related to Pupil-Teacher-Ratios, buildings & infra, working-days, working-hours
- rational deployment of teachers - not only maintain State/ District/ Block wise averages - balance urban-rural postings
- prohibition of teacher deployments for non-teaching work, other than census, elections & disaster relief

### Impacts
- **Enrollment Surge** - Gross enrollment ratio in age group of 6-14 years 96.7%(2009) to 98.7%(2023)
- **Infrastructure** - Toilets in **98.5%**, drinking water available in **96%**
- **Equity** - 25% EWS quota benefitted 2 crore+ children, girl enrollment increased
- **Teacher Recruitment** - 10 lakh+ TET-qualified teachers, National Initiative for School Heads' & Teachers' Holistic Advancement (NISHTHA) trained teachers increased to 50 lakh+

### Conclusion
RTE Act was historical legal milestone that shifted education from a constitutional promise to an enforceable right.
impressive gains in access, equity, infra improvements are evident, but quality of learning & effective implementation continue to be critical challenges

in journey towards transformative education, focus must move from mere attendance towards meaningful learning & accountability.

*Bridging this gap will determine whether India succeeds in translating Right to Education to education of rights*
