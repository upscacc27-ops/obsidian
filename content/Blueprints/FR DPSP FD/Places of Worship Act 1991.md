
### Context
SC refused to entertain further pleas challenging Place of Worship Act, 1991 - it is a landmark legislation that prohibits conversion of religious places & mandates maintenance of their character as of 15 Aug 1947

### Background, Aim & Major Provisions
- Background - Escalation of *Babri Masjid- Ram Janambhoomi* dispute,  Hindu organisations like *Vishwa Hindu Parishad* contested *Gyanvapi* Mosque, Varanasi & *Shahi Idgah* in Mathura too
- Sept 1991, PV Narsimha Rao govt passed a law freezing status of all places of worship as of 15/Aug/1947. 
- This law excluded Ayodhya structure as it was already under litigation
- Aim - Ensure preservation of religious character of places of worship
- Major Provisions
	- Section 3 - **Prohibition of conversion** - either from 1 religion to another or within the same religion, fully or partially
	- Section 4(1) - **Maintenance of actual religious character**
	- Section 4 - **Exceptions** - does not apply to ancient & historical monuments & archeological sites covered under *Ancient Monuments & Archeological Sites and Remains Act 1958*, does not apply to disputes already resolved, conversions occurred before law came into effect, does not apply to Ram Janambhoomi-Babri Masjid in Ayodhya
	- Section 6 - **Penalties** - max imprisonment of 3 years or fines, for violating the act

### Conclusion
Act was designed to prevent communal conflicts & legal disputes over religious sites. Rise in petitions seeking surveys of Mosques have challenged its implementation. SC's intervention & government's response will determine whether the act remains effective in preserving secularism & communal harmony
