### Context 
Committee on Political Affairs, headed by PM decided caste enumeration as a part of next Census exercise, last time done in 1931. Since then only SC and ST have been counted

### Difference between Census, SECC, Caste Survey

| Census                                               | Caste Census (SECC)                              | Caste Survey                    |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------- |
| backed by Census Act, 1948                           | no statutory backing                             | no statutory backing            |
| Socio-economic data of SCs, STs collected & released | SE data of OBCs collected in 2011 - not released | State Govt conduct periodically |
| All data confidential                                | used to grant/restrict benefits                  | informed policy making          |

### Way forward
- Standardized Classification based on sociological & anthropological data - nomenclature challenges
- Train personnel - sensitive nature of effort - respectful engagement - voluntary participation - data confidentiality 
- Data accuracy & reliability - local leaders - build trust - robust verification mechanisms
- Public Awareness - address privacy concerns - encourage accurate participation
- Strong data protection protocols
- Avoid misuse for political, divisive or partisan purposes - not a tool for deepening social divisions

### Conclusion
several apprehensions about consequences of caste census, however up to govt and civil society to ensure it is used for social harmony rather than fragmentation. Golden tool for better power sharing & deepening democracy
