### Introduction
refers to imprisonment to prevent a possible commitment of crime on the grounds of suspicion
Leads to detention without trial, purpose is not to punish, but to prevent a crime in near future

### Constitutional, Legislative Provisions
- [[Art 22 Protection against arrest and detention]] 
- Legislations
	- [[1980 National Security Act]]
	- COFEPOSA 1974 - to maintain & improve foreign exchange & deter illegal trade
	- TADA 1985 - to successfully counter-act terrorism & violent acts
	- [[Unlawful Activities Prevention Act]]

###  Arguments & Reforms

| Supporting                                                                       | Against                                                                                            | Reforms                                                                     |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Pre-emptive security - averts crimes                                             | Arbitrariness, misuse - broad & vague grounds - political gain                                     | Clear & narrow grounds, full disclosure to detainee                         |
| public order in volatile situations - elections, insurgency, communal volatility | anti-ethical to innocent till proven guilty                                                        | Use digital tracking to enhance transparency - timely audit                 |
| expediated response - no delays in prosecution, trial                            | Although advisory boards required - could be concerns of independence - lack of judicial oversight | time-bound detention & mandatory & quick judicial review                    |
| Stops black marketing (2020 Oxygen hoarding)                                     | Impact on minorities, political activists, dissenters                                              | mandatory report submission on number & nature of such cases                |
|                                                                                  |                                                                                                    | Align laws with International Human Rights standards & Constitutional Ethos |
### Committee Recommendations
- NCRWC, 2002 -recommended maximum period for detention  = 6months, advisory board should include chairman + 2 serving judges of HC

### Judicial intervention
- [[1983 Alijav v DM, Dhanbad]]
- [[1997 Anukul Chandra Pradhan v UOI]]
- [[1994 AK Gopalan v State of Madras]]

### Conclusion
- balances national security and individual liberty but requires constant vigilance against misuse
- Periodic legal reforms, transparency, judicial oversight, human rights protections required to ensure that it serves as a protective tool, not a weapon of oppression
