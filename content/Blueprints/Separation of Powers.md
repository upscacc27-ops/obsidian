### Introduction
Doctrine of Separation of powers means dividing government authority into three branches - legislature, executive & judiciary. 
Main aim to prevent concentration of power into one organ and prevent arbitrary rule.

### Origin & Evolution
- first clearly expressed by *Montesquieu*, who believed that *individual liberty is best protected when govt. powers are divided among its branches*
- first followed by the US Constitution - with strict separation of power between Congress, President & Judiciary
- Indian founding fathers also discussed the principle and there was a proposal to include it as a DPSP but did not adopt. [[Art 50 Separation of judiciary from executive]] was added.

### Indian System of Checks & Balances
- India instead follows a system of checks & balances, with no strict separation of powers & allows functional overlap between different organs. 
- Being a parliamentary democracy, where the executive holds collective responsibility to the Lok Sabha, strict separation is not feasible in India. 
- Moreover, a young developing country like India cannot afford friction & gridlock often seen in American democracy, due to conflicts between branches

### Judicial intervention
- Ram Jawaya Kapoor v State of Punjab, 1955 - SC held that Indian constitution does not provide strict separation of powers, recognized functional separation, but stated that separation is not absolute
- [[1967 IC Golaknath v State of Punjab]]
- [[1975 Indira Nehru Gandhi v Raj Narain]]

### Issues with Current Indian System of Checks & Balances
- Weakening of legislative checks - Parliamentary debates & Committee oversight can become less robust, affective executive's accountability
- Judiciary-Executive tensions in appointments - 99th CAA - NJAC struck down by Supreme Court, limits broader participation 
- Judicial overreach & activism - Expansive interpretations enter policy domains
- Frequent use of Ordinance making power - bypass legislative debates
- Judicial vacancies  & delays - no timely justice delivery

### Way forward
- Strengthening Institutional Accountability - clear boundaries  & Mutual restraint
- Revitalize parliamentary oversight - opposition participation, greater scrutiny
- Transparent Judicial appointments - inter institution dialogue
- Restrained use of ordinance making power
- Promote Constitutional culture- rule of law, respect checks & balances
