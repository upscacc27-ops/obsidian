### Introduction
organized group of individuals who share common interests and seek to influence government policy or decision making without attempting to gain political power
they represent the interests of a particular section of society like farmers, workers, industry/social workers


### Methods employed
- **Lobbying** - Confederation of Indian Industry (CII) meeting govt. officials to discuss industrial & tax policies
- **Public protests, demonstrations** - *Bharatiya Kisan Union* on MSP Laws
- **Strikes/Bandhs** - nationwide strikes by All India Trade Union Congress (AITUC) for better labour rights
- **Media Campaigns** - Centre for Science & Environment - pollution & climate concerns
- **Petitions & memorandums** - Indian Medical Association + other professional groups, submitting memorandums
- **Coalition building** - social movements like Narmada Bachao Andolan(NBA) worked with multiple civic society groups


### Different types of groups
- Agrarian / Farmer orgs - issues related to crop prices, MSP, irrigation, farm loans (ex - *All India Kisan Sabha*, *Bharatiya Kisan Union*, *Bharatiya Kisan Sangh*, *Samyukt Kisan Morcha*, *Kisan Mazdoor Morcha*)
- Business / Industrial groups - represent interests of industries & business owners, problems related to taxes, investment, trade, industrial growth (ex - Confederation of Indian Industry, All India Manufacturers Organization, Federation of Indian Chambers of Commerce & Industry of India)
- Trade Unions - better wages, job security, safe working conditions (ex - Bharatiya Mazdoor sangh, Indian National Trade Union Congress (INTUC), All India Trade Union Congress (AITUC), Hind Mazdoor Sabha)
- Environmental groups - protect nature, forests, wildlife, natural resources (ex - Chipko movement, Centre for Science & Environment, Wildlife Trust of India)
- Caste groups - social justice, prevention of atrocities (ex- Dalit panther, Jat Sabha, Vanniyar Sangam, Harijan Sewak Sangh)
- Religious groups - protect beliefs, traditions & practices of a religion (ex - Rashtriya SwayamSewak Sangh (RSS), Vishwa Hindu Parishad, Jamaat-e-Islami)
- Professional groups - (ex - Indian Medical Association (IMA), Bar Council of India)
- Tribal groups - ex - National Social Council of Nagaland, Tribal National Volunteers - Tripura
- Ideology based - collective public good, specific value systems (Ex - Narmada Bachao Andolan, India against Corruption)

### Difference between Pressure groups & Political Parties


| Pressure groups                                                                                                         | Political parties                             |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| influence govt policies from outside                                                                                    | contest elections - capture power             |
| specific groups/ interests                                                                                              | broader ideology - all socio-economic aspects |
| informal & spontaneous nature (formal ones if registered under Society Registration Act, 1860 or Trade Unions Act 1926) | formal, structured, permanent                 |
| lobbying, protests, strikes etc.                                                                                        | Constitutional means, rallies, debates        |
| accountability to members                                                                                               | accountable to whole electorate               |

### Conclusion
In a democracy, pressure groups act as the voice of the people by bringing the concerns of different sections of society to attention of the govt.
