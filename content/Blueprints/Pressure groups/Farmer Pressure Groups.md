### Method employed
- Mass mobilization - Dharna, Road blockades, rail blockades to create visible disruptions & force administrative attention Ex - Shambu, Kanauri borders
- Direct Lobbying - negotiations with Ministry of Agriculture & NITI AYOG to influence Union Budget Ex - Sugarcane Farmer Associations in UP/Maharashtra secure annual hikes in Fair & Remunerative Price (FRP)
- Agnostic Political mobilization - leverage vote bank by issuing Kisan Manifestos
- Digital Advocacy - 2026 Mazdoor Kisan Parliament - Trending hashtags highlighting asymmetric nature of India-US Trade framework
- Judicial Activism - PILs challenging executive decisions - land acquisitions, environmental clearances or GMO needs
- Long marches - visual impact ex - AIKS led Maharashtra Long March for recognition of tribal rights
### Need 
- small individual farmers lack bargaining power - collective strength to negotiate
- raising MSP issue to mitigate market volatility
- prevent top-down legislations - Policies vetted at grassroot levels by farmers before passing
- push for faster claim settlements under PFMBY, safety net for vulnerable crops amid extreme weather conditions
- combating the debt trap - institutionalized lending - NSSO 77th round survey - 50% of Indian agricultural households under debt
- empowering women farmers - NSSO report - 85% women in rural farming, 13% own land

### Significance
- Farmer PGs like *Samyukta Kisan Morcha* articulate rural voices & demands like legal guarantee of C2 + 50% MSP, gathering national interest
- safety valve - check on executive overreach - Bharatiya Kisan Union has historically acted as a watchdog, forcing withdrawal of land acquisition bills
- bridge information gap- educate about global trade, newer tech, Shetkari Sanghatana, founded in 1979, Maharashtra shifted focus from demanding subsidies to remunerative practices
- foster a sense of community beyond regional borders - All India Kisan Sabha
- Influencing the fiscal roadmap - lobbying during pre-budget consultations, ensure sufficient fund allocation for Agri-infra 

### Challenges
- Regional imbalance - mobilization highly concentrated in green-belt states (Pb, HR, West UP), central & western states (BH, OR) less organized
- Political elements - used as vote-bank tools - loss of credibility
- moral v fiscal conflict - loan waivers (Rs. 35k crore in Maharashtra 2026) relieve farmers' distress, but harm credit discipline
- internal fragmentation - ideology conflicts - reduced bargaining power
- exclusion of landless farmers - laborers & tenants
- technology & knowledge resistance - reforms like Genetically modified crops & Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) resisted due to fear of corporate hijacking

### Way forward
- Ashok Dalwai Committee's recommendations - pre-legislative consultations with farmer unions. make them formal stakeholders in policy drafting to prevent top-down friction
- broaden agenda - beyond MSP, effects on state economy, resource allocation
- establish agri-tribunals - @ district level to settle disputes
- establish a consistent & collective farmer identity - Karl Marx compared farmers to a sack of potatoes, as they only organize in response to specific issues
- Avoid political affiliations - 


### Conclusion
Evolution of farmer pressure groups reflects a maturing rural democracy transitioning from local agitation to global identities. 
To achieve *Viksit Bharata* by 2047, the focus of PGs must shift from confrontation to consultation as recommended by Ashok Dalwai committee
