### Significance
- Bureaucracy lacks market data & expertise of business sector- bridge gaps,  ex - NASSCOM provides critical data on global AI trends  & cybersecurity - helps is policy making related to technologies - Digital India Act
- clearing house for diverse industrial demands - effective articulation Ex - CII & FICCI aggregate concerns from varied sectors & present a unified Pre-Budget memorandum to the Finance Ministry
- MSME Advocacy - shield against big conglomerates (FISME lobbied for TReDS(Trade Receivables Discounting System)) to solve problem of delayed payments to small enterprises
- Policy monitoring & feedback loop - Watchdogs on implementation
- Promote Ease of Doing Business - identify chokepoints in bureaucracy such as Inspector Raj ex- ASSOCHAM's feedback on Insolvency & Bankruptcy Code (IBC) led to faster resolution timelines, improving capital recycling in banking sector

### Challenges
- Risk of Crony Capitalism - promotion of interests of politically proximate firms - as highlighted by World Inequality Report 2026, lobbying by top 1% leads to regulatory capture
- Inequality of access - Large groups like CII & FICCI have direct access to govt. 63 million MSMEs contributing 30% to GDP struggle to have voices heard
- Narrow motives - lobby against stringent environmental norms or labor welfare laws to increase profits - ex- extensive pushback against Extensive Producer Responsibility (EPR) for plastic use
- Lack of internal democracy - large corporates dictate agenda
- Sectoral imbalance - high-growth sectors (IT/Semiconductors) etc. have louder voices than traditional employment heavy-sectors (leather/textiles)
- Opaque funding - India lacks formal Lobbying Disclosure Act - difficult to track down corporate interests driving legislative actions

### Way forward
- bridge the big-small divide - formalize mentorship-based lobbying - large industries' policy demands bundled with protections for small MSMEs
- Voluntarily publish policy white papers & agendas
- incorporate larger public interests - rather than narrow profits
- Social security - especially for gig & casual workers
- Neutral Functional Autonomy of Competition Commission of India to ensure a level playing field

### Conclusion
BPGs must function as a bridge between the market & governance, so that the journey towards $10 trillion economy becomes a shared triumph
