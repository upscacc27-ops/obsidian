>[!quote] Supreme Court
>AGI is a "friend, philosopher & guide of the Court" rather than just a government representative

### Introduction
AGI is the highest law officer of the country, plays a crucial role in advising the govt. & defending laws before courts.
Position is created under the constitution to ensure that executive functions within legal boundaries

- inspired by the British legal system, where the chief legal advisor to the Crown is the Attorney General for England & Wales

### Constitutional Provisions
- [[Art 76 Appointment of AGI]] establishes the office
- Art 76(1) provides that the President shall appoint a person qualified to be a judge of the SC to be AGI

### Powers & Functions
- Art 76(2) - acts as a chief legal advisor to GOI. Advises the President & Union govt. on constitutional matters, opines on bills, treaties, ordinances & performs legal duties assigned by the President
- Art 76(3) - AGI has the right to audience in all courts of India, can represent GOI anywhere
- Art 88 - right to speak & participate in the proceedings of both houses, including Joint sitting & Parliamentary Committees. He/She cannot vote.
- Art 105 - gives AGI same parliamentary privileges & immunities as MPs when he speaks in the house. 

### Challenges
- Lack of fixed tenure - holds office during pleasure of the President. unlike SC judges, CAG etc.
- Conflict of interest (private practice allowed) - allowing private practice can dilute public confidence in the AGI's independence as an impartial guardian of the law
- Perception of political bias - government's advocate - creates tension between loyalty to the executive & upholding constitutional morality
- No clear removal procedure- no specified grounds or impeachment process, reduces stability & security
- Not accountable to Parliament - Cannot be questioned during question hour - unlike MPs
- Bypassing the AGI - Govt sometimes appoints special senior advocates - as in [[2017 Justice KS Puttaswamy v UOI]] the govt. appointed senior advocates to argue detailed technical & constitutional aspects of Aadhar before SC
- Advisory hierarchy - all official requests for AGI's advice must be routed through Ministry of Law, delays in urgent matters
- Misuse of Contempt cases- AGI's power of contempt misused by ruling parties

### Reforms
- Provide fixed tenure - institutional continuity, stability, security ex - UK's AG of England & Wales has a stable political tenure linked to term of the Govt
- Restrict private practice - ex - US - AG heads the US Dept. of Justice & cannot engage in private practice
- Clear code of conduct - define neutrality, transparency & conflict standards ex - [[2nd ARC]] recommended ethical governance & institutional accountability for const & non-const bodies
- Strengthen Parliamentary oversight - hold AGI accountable, periodic review of Legal policy
- Independent Litigation Wing - Law Commission of India has suggested reducing govt litigation through institutional reforms in AGI's office
- Clear Separation between advisory & prosecution roles - US - AG heads federal prosecutions separately through Dept. of Justice
- Annual Public Legal Report - major cases handled & major opinions issued
- Clarify removal procedure & grounds - [[2nd ARC]] recommended clarity in removal mechanisms

### Important related SC judgement
- PUDR v UOI 1982 - SC emphasized law officers representing the govt must act fairly. Govt lawyers including the AGI are expected to assist the court fairly, not merely defend at any cost

### Conclusion
Strengthening transparency & accountability can make office of AGI more balanced & trust worthy.
In a democracy like India, AGI must serve not just the govt but also the spirit of the constitution
