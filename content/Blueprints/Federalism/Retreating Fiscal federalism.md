### Context
Sothern states have been raising issues of reduced devolution despite higher contribution in Gross Tax Revenues

### Introduction
- Fiscal federalism refers to division of financial powers & responsibilities between central & state governments

### Constitutional Provisions

- [[7th Schedule]] - specific tax bases for both levels of govt
- [[Art 270 Taxes levied & distributed between Centre & states]] - distribution of net tax proceeds collected by Centre
- [[Art 280 Finance Commission]] recommends sharing of tax revenue & grant-in aids
- [[Art 275 Grants from Union to certain States]] involves discretionary transfers from Centre to States

### Examples of cooperative fiscal federalism

- [[Goods & Service Tax]] - [[101st CAA 2016]] - historic example of cooperative fiscal federalism, transformed India's indirect taxation landscape
- [[FRBM Act 2003]] - aims to promote fiscal discipline at center and state levels. 21 states enacted their own FRBM acts, incentivized by debt & interest relief provided by 12th FC. 
- Performance-based grants - incentivize states to achieve development targets, promotes competitive & cooperative federalism

### Reforms
- review 16th FC devolution - ensure equitable distribution
- Rationalization of public expenditure - less ties more autonomy
- addressing GST related concerns - anomalies favoring consuming states like UP, Bihar instead of producing states like TN, Gujarat
- Revisit Art 246, 7th Schedule
- Strengthen taxation powers of the 3rd tier, achieve fiscal federalism in true sense
- reduction on borrowing constraints
- reduction in cesses & surcharges
- More transparency  & predictability, less discretion & discrimination

### Conclusion
Fiscal federalism seeks to balance national unity with regional autonomy through a constitutionally defined & dynamic system of resource sharing.
Strengthen cooperative & transparent fiscal relations between Centre & states to secure equitable development & effective governance
