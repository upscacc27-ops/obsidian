### Introduction
Finance Commission is a Constitutional body constituted by the President, every 5years
Aims to make recommendation on Centre-State Fiscal Relations
Report of 16th FC (chairman : Dr. Arvind Panagariya) was tabled 1 Feb, 2026 for 2026-27 to 2030-31
### Key Recommendations
- **Vertical Devolution** - share of states in divisible pool = 41%, same as 15th FC
- **Horizontal Devolution** - distribution among states, formula is based on 
	- Income Distance
	- Population
	- Demographic performance
	- Area
	- Forest
	- Contribution to GDP
	- ~~Tax & Fiscal effort~~ (removed in 16th, present in 15th)
- **Grants-in-aid** - 16th FC recommended grants worth Rs. 9.47 lakh crore over 5 years, for
	- urban, rural local bodies
	- disaster management
	- discontinued grants - Revenue deficit grants, Sector-specific grants, State-specific grants
- **Grants for Local bodies**
	- rural - 4.4 lakh crore
	- urban - 3.6 lakh crore
	- divided into
		- basic(80%) -  50% of basic is tied to solid waste management & 50% to water management
		- performance based(20%) - state performance grants (upon meeting a minimum benchmark for state transfer to local bodies)  + local performance grants(on meeting minimum benchmark for own source revenue growth)
	- special infra grants & urbanization premium grants
	- All local body grants is made available on basis of
		1. Constitution of local bodies as per the Constitution
		2. Publication of qualified & audited accounts of local bodies in public domain
		3. Timely Constitution of State FC
- **Disaster Management grants** - Rs 2,04,401 crore for State Disaster Relief & Management funds, Cost sharing = 90:10 for Himalayan & NE states, 75:25 elsewhere
- **Fiscal roadmap** - recommended Centre should bring down fiscal deficit to 3.5% of GDP by end of cycle. annual fiscal deficit limit for states to be 3% of State GDP, discontinue off-budget borrowings, include them in fiscal deficit & state debt
- **Power sector** - privatization of power distribution companies (DISCOMs), to shield the private investor from debt after takeover, special purpose vehicle needs to be created to warehouse the debt, eventual repayment using funds from Special assistance schemes, only once privatization is complete
- **Subsidy Expenditure** - review & rationalize subsidies - unregulated cash transfers tend to have untargeted beneficiaries, set a clear exclusion criteria
- **PSE reforms** - review & closure of 308 inactive State PSEs, formulation of State level disinvestment policy to target underperforming & inactive PSEs

| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Rewards economic development - GDP criterion - developed states felt penalized earlier                                                                                                                                                                                         | Vertical devolution stagnation - 18/28 states requested at least 50%, especially post GST                                                                 |
| Urbanization Focus - 16th FC acknowledged India's future growth lies in cities<br>Urbanization premiums - 1 time incentive for states to merge semi-urban villages into larger ULBs<br>Special Infra Component - dedicated funding for freshwater management in midsize cities | Cess & Surcharges loophole - collected by centre, not shared with states.<br>2013-19 :  93-95% was taxes + 5-7 % was cess & surcharge, now it is 11%      |
| Enforcing fiscal discipline - end of offbudget borrowings - true picture of debt<br>rationalize subsidies - not a general populist tool                                                                                                                                        | Impact on poorer states - Income distance parameter's weightage reduced from 45% to 42.5%, violated economic convergence                                  |
| Empowerment of Local governance - performance linked grants                                                                                                                                                                                                                    | Discontinuation of Revenue Deficit Grants - affects Hills & other Special category states, which have revenue deficit due to lack of favorable topography |
| Transparency - Centre to annually disclose tax data certified by CAG to ensure transparent use of divisive pool                                                                                                                                                                | Warning against freebies seen as ideological intervention into state welfare politics                                                                     |

### Conclusion
16th FC is to play a crucial role in strengthening fiscal federalism by ensuring fair distribution of resources.
it aims to promote balanced economic growth, transparency & cooperative governance across India
