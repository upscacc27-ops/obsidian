### Introduction
arises due to political, fiscal & institutional disagreements between Centre & States

### Causes
- Political Causes 
	- ideological conflicts between different parties
	- electoral competition = mistrust
	- rise of regional parties
	- political centralization threat to state autonomy
- Institutional  Causes 
	- Governors appointed by Centre
	- [[Art 356 President's Rule]]
	- Central control over AIS
	- Concurrent List - overlapping jurisdiction
- Fiscal Causes
	- GST = reduced independent taxing power of States
	- GST Compensation delays
	- Frequent changes in GST devolution criteria - debates on equitable distribution
- Central Agencies
	- CBI - states withdrew general consent under DSPE Act
	- ED investigations
	- allegedly used as tools of political pressure
- Judicial
	- [[Art 131 SC's original jurisdiction]] states approach SC

### Negative impacts
- Weakens cooperative federalism
- administrative inefficiencies & governance paralysis - delays in policy making, prolonged conflicts
- institutional conflicts - Governor v State legislatures
- Fiscal conflicts - GST Compensation disputes

### Judicial intervention
- [[1973 Keshavananda Bharti v UOI]] - federalism is part of basic structure
- [[1994 S. R. Bommai v UOI]] - restricted misuse of [[Art 356 President's Rule]], use subject to Judicial Review
- Govt. of NCT of Delhi v Union of India, 2018 - SC emphasized cooperative federalism & harmony

### Way forward

| Reform                                                                             | Backing                                                                             |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Strengthen Inter State Council as permanent dialogue & dispute resolution body     | [[Sarkaria Commission, 1988]]                                                       |
| Reform Role of Governor - neutral, just use of discretionary powers                | [[Puncchi Commission 2010]] &<br>[[2016 Nebam Rabia v Deputy Speaker]]              |
| Predictable, transparent, equitable tax devolution                                 | [[Art 280 Finance Commission]] promoting objective criteria                         |
| Federal institutions - GST Council - consensus based decision making               | [[2022 Union of India v Mohit Minerals]] affirmed cooperative nature of GST Council |
| Strengthen judicial resolution of disputes                                         | [[Art 131 SC's original jurisdiction]]                                              |
| Institutionalize structured consultation with states before major policy decisions | [[Puncchi Commission 2010]]                                                         |

### Conclusion
Indian federalism is a dynamic system shaped by healthy competition & confrontation. Excessive confrontation can undermine federal harmony. Strengthening cooperative mechanisms & ensuring institutional neutrality essential to maintain balance.
