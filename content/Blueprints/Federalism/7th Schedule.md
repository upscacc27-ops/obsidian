### Introduction
- it delineates the distribution of powers & responsibilities between Center & state govts
- ensures balanced federal structure, clarifies jurisdictions
- [[Art 246 Distribution of Legislative Powers - 7th Schedule]] mentions 3 lists


| Union List                                                                             | State List                                                                        | Concurrent List                                               |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| matters of national importance                                                         | matters of regional importance                                                    | matters that need national standards but local implementation |
| Parliament has exclusive legislative power                                             | States have exclusive legislative power, generally                                | both can make legislations                                    |
| ensure uniformity & national unity                                                     | laws tailored to socio-economic needs                                             |                                                               |
| Key subjects : Defence, Railways, Foreign affairs, Banking, Atomic Energy, Citizenship | Key Subjects: Agriculture, Police, Public order, Health & sanitation, Local govt. | Key Subjects: Education, forests, Civil Laws, Trade unions    |
| Originally 97, now 100                                                                 | Originally 66, now 61                                                             | Originally 47, now 52                                         |


### Significance & issues

| Significance                                                                               | Issues                                                                                                                                                   |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Clear Division of Powers - no jurisdictional overlap - avoids redundant effort & conflicts | Overlapping jurisdiction - especially Concurrent List                                                                                                    |
| National Unity (united front) + Federal autonomy(diversity) - prevents 1-size-fits-all     | Redundant entries - colonial relics - structure taken from GOI Act, 1935                                                                                 |
| Decentralization of authority, empowerment of states to legislate according to their needs | Unitary tilt - already more subjects in Union list + [[Art 248 Residuary Powers]] & [[Art 249 Power to legislate on state subject in national interest]] |
| Concurrent list - cooperative federalism                                                   | Slow & Complex amendment process - political consensus - difficult to adapt                                                                              |
| Residuary powers - no legislative vacuum                                                   | Modern subject challenges - no clear entries for Climate Change, Global pandemics, Cyber Security, Data privacy                                          |
|                                                                                            | Differences in law implementation across states - poor coordination especially in cross-border transport etc.                                            |
### Way forward
- [[Sarkaria Commission, 1988]] - 
	- minimize overlaps & ambiguities, clear definitions
	- Potentially reduce Union list, promote state autonomy
	- before legislating on concurrent list - consult with states - prevent legislative surprise
	- residuary powers - except taxation - should be moved to concurrent list
	- establish Inter state council - structured dialogue mechanism
- [[Puncchi Commission 2010]] - 
	- refine the schedule - add contemporary subjects
	- formal discussion mechanism between Centre & States before legislation on Concurrent List
	- empowered roles of states
- Outcome-based lists rather than subject-based - (Forests -> Climate Change Mitigation), framework where Union sets national goal, states implement & achieve
- Clear Grey zone - Technical Standing Committees & Expert committees under ISC before deciding on a new subject
- Periodic Reviews - Removal, addition & movement of entries according to contemporary realities
- Flexibility in implementing concurrent list - improve competitive federalism

### Conclusion
Need to move away from water-tight-separations to shared sovereignty, in an inter-connected economy, Union & States are no longer competitors but partners
