
### Introduction
Special Category Status is classification given by Centre to assist states in development that face geographic or socio-economic disadvantage

### History & Introduction
introduced in 1969, on recommendation of 5th FC, based on Gadgil formula
was developed to assist certain states in development & fast-tracked growth, states that faced historical economic or geographic disadvantages

### Gadgil formula
- Difficult & Hilly Terrain
- Low population density or sizeable tribal population
- Strategic Location (Border states)
- Economic & Infrastructural backwardness
- Non-viable state finances

14th FC removed special status, except for NE(Assam, Nagaland, Manipur, Meghalaya, Sikkim, Tripura, Arunachal) & Himalayan states (Himachal, Uttarakhand) & Telangana
14th FC recommended tax devolution of 42% instead of existing 32% for these states to fill resource gap
