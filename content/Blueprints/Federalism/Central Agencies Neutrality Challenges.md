### Introduction
CBI, ED are central investigative agencies established under Union laws to investigate Corruption, Money Laundering and other economic crimes. 
However, since Police is a state subject, these agencies investigating in states have created tensions regarding federal autonomy & institutional independence.


### Issues
- Police is a state subject, central agencies investigating - concerns of federal overreach, Punjab, West Bengal & Maharashtra withdrew general consent from CBI under DSPE Act
- Agencies work under Union ministries, concerns of neutrality & political influence, SC termed *CBI as a caged parrot speaking in the voice of its masters* in Coal Scam Case, 2013
- Courts can authorize agencies without state consent, as in State of West Bengal v CPDR, 2010

### Judicial intervention
- [[1997 Vineet Narain v UOI]] - fixed minimum tenure to CBI director & independence of CBI
- 2014 Subramanian Swamy v Director CBI - struck down Single Directive Provision of prior permission
- 2018 Common Cause v UOI - Appointment & removal of CBI Director must be done by a committee of PM, LoP, CJI
- 2022 Vijay Madanlal Choudhary v UOI - Upheld constitutional validity of PMLA & ED's investigative powers
- 2023 Pankaj Bansal v UOI - ED must provide written grounds of arrest
- 2023 Common Cause v UOI - SC held repeated extensions of ED Director's tenure improper

### Conclusion
CBI & ED play a crucial role in investigating corruption & financial crimes. However their functioning within state jurisdiction has raised federal concerns.
Ensuring institutional independence & accountability is essential to maintain cooperative federalism
