### Context
Recent abrogation of [[Art 370 Special Provision wrt J&K]] has highlighted the nature of asymmetric federalism that exists in India, proponents of Art 370 have argued that abrogation goes against asymmetric federalism as envisaged by our founders

### Introduction
Asymmetric federalism is based on unequal powers & relationships in political, administrative & fiscal spheres between federal units

### Types
- Vertical asymmetry - [[Art 3 Formation of new states, Increase, Decrease, Alter boundary or names]], [[Art 356 President's Rule]], [[Art 248 Residuary Powers]], Single citizenship, Governor appointents
- Horizontal asymmetry - Art 371 - 371J - special provisions to some states, Schedule 5, Schedule 6, [[Art 239AA Special provisions wrt Delhi]]
- Fiscal Asymmetry - Devolution of 41% of Taxes, criteria for devolution, Centrally Sponsored Schemes (special category status for states)

### Significance & Challenges


| Significance                                                           | Challenges                                                 |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Addresses historical inequalities with certain regions                 | regionalism & separatism                                   |
| border states given large autonomy for management of internal security | perception of favoritism, imbalance in resource allocation |
| empowerment of marginalized communities by providing greater autonomy  | varying levels of cultural & linguistic protections        |
| address state specific issues                                          | preferential funding - economic disparities                |
| substantial equality - decentralization as per the requirements        | selective application - political motivations              |
### Conclusion
Indian founding fathers recognized the need for asymmetric federalism due to distinctive cultural differences in the country, permitting varying degrees of self rule within a scheme of shared rule
However due to political considerations & in the name of greater autonomy, these provisions have been misused, aim must be to protect the core idea of these principles
