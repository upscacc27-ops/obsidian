### Introduction
These disputes are disagreements over territorial boundaries. Most disputes emerged after [[States Reorganisation Act of 1956]]. Colonial era demarcations & linguistic reorganizations contribute to these conflicts
They continue to affect governance & federal harmony in India

### Major disputes
- Karnataka - Maharashtra (Belgaum)
- Haryana - Himachal Pradesh (Parwanoo region)
- Himachal Pradesh - Ladakh (Sarchu)
- Assam - Nagaland - Arunachal - Mizoram

### Constitutional provisions & institutional mechanisms
- [[Art 131 SC's original jurisdiction]] - states can approach SC for dispute resolution
- [[Art 263 Provisions wrt Inter State Council]] provides for an Inter State Council to promote coordination
- [[States Reorganisation Act of 1956]] created Zonal Councils
- [[Art 3 Formation of new states, Increase, Decrease, Alter boundary or names]]

### Root causes
- Colonial demarcations - British era boundaries - create conflicting interpretations
- Linguistic reorganization of states - [[States Reorganisation Act of 1956]] led to competing territorial claims
- Competition for resources - economic potential of forests, tourism zones etc
- Political opportunism - mobilization of regional sentiments
- Geographical factors - shifting rivers, hills complicate demarcations
- Limited Institutional use - ISC & Zonal councils - reactive rather than proactive approach

These disputes challenge cooperative federalism & national unity
require dialogue, political will & effective use of constitutional institutions for peaceful & timely resolution. 
Their resolution is essential for stability, long term peace & development
