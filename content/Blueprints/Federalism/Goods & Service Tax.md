### Introduction
GST implemented on 1 July 2017, through 101st CAA, 2016, fundamentally restructured India's fiscal federal framework. By subsuming multiple indirect taxes levied both by Centre & states, GST replaced a fragmented origin-based tax regime with a harmonized, destination-based system based upon shared legislation & Institutionalized Centre-State coordination

### Background
- Kelkar Task Force on Indirect Taxes, 2003 first recommended a comprehensive GST
- Subsequent negotiations culminated in 101st GST, 2016 - accompanied by a compensation assurance guaranteeing 14% annual growth to states for 5 years

### Objectives
- Unified national market - single regime - removed internal trade barriers
- removal of cascading taxes 
- digital transparency - GSTN based compliance improve formalization, curbs evasion
- cooperative framework - [[Art 279A GST Council]] for joint decision making
- revenue stability - enhanced buoyancy 

### Judicial Intervention
- [[2022 Mohit Minerals v UOI]] - 
	- GST Council recommendations are not binding on Parliament or State legislatures
	- [[Art 246A Special provisions wrt GST]] grants simultaneous powers

### Conclusion
GST marks the most profound restructuring of India's fiscal federalism since adoption of Constitution, replaces fragmented competitive taxation with institutionalized cooperative governance 
However, its long-term sustainability depends on restoring fiscal trust, balancing harmonization with state autonomy, ensuring fiscal federalism remains substantive rather than procedural
