
### Context
Centre has extended tenure of Krishna Water disputes Tribunal till July 2026.

### Introduction
recurrent federal issue in India, lot of similar disputes festering among various states for a very long time without a possible solution in sight


### Provisions to settle such disputes
- Constitutional  - Schedule 7 puts water use into state list but, inter-state rivers into Union List, [[Art 262 Adjudication of disputes related to water & river valleys]] empowers parliament to adjudicate such disputes & exclude court jurisdiction
- Statutory - River Boards Act, 1956 provides for advisory boards to promote coordinated river basin development & Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956 provides for setting up tribunals

### Conclusion
these disputes persist due to political mobilization & weak institutions. Both Centre & state must prioritize national interest over regional politics
with rising climate induced water stress, timely resolution is essential for optimal use & federal stability
