### Context
Climate activist Sonam Wangchuk, has been at forefront of demand for statehood & 6th schedule status for Ladakh, and has been detained under [[1980 National Security Act]]
Similar demands have been made for Arunachal Pradesh & Manipur previously

### Historical roots & provisions of schedule
- Roots - Colonial policies that recognized distinct nature of tribal populations, to address their discontent, GOI Act 1935 introduced excluded & semi-excluded areas, which led to 5th & 6th schedules
- Provisions - greater autonomy to Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura  & Mizoram, 
	- ADC (Autonomous District Councils) under 6th schedule manage land use, inheritance laws & social customs
	- ADC hold legislative, executive & financial power - ability to collect taxes & manage local resources, ADCs can make laws that supersede state laws, on approval of Governor

### Reasons behind Ladakh's demand
- Political representation & autonomy - erstwhile J&K Leg Ass had 4 MLAs from Ladakh, now administration is completely in hands of bureaucrats.
- Lack of Local Employment Opportunities - Absence of public services commission
- Preservation of cultural identity - Gujjars, Bakarwals, Bots, Changpas, Baltis etc.
- Preservation of a fragile glacial ecosystem - Mining & industrialization pose a threat to Alpine Meadows - natural habitat to rare & endangered species
- Change in domicile policy of J&K
- Need for ADCs & empowerment of Ladakh Autonomous Hill Development Councils (LAHDC)

### Conclusion
6th schedule represents framework for safeguarding tribal identity through autonomous governance in tribal areas.
While it strengthens culture preservation & self rule, effectiveness depends upon addressing issues of overlapping authorities, inclusivity & transparent functioning
