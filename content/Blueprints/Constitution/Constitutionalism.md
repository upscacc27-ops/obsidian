### Introduction
Constitutionalism refers to the idea that government power must be limited, regulated & exercises only in accordance with the constitution.

### Origin
- can be traced back to *Magna Carta* of 1215, England
- institutionalization of a written constitutional supremacy by the US in 1787, further strengthened this idea

### Types
- Negative Constitutionalism - constitution primarily acts as a mechanism to limit arbitrary authority of the state.
- Positive Constitutionalism - constitution is a tool to make state power effective & purposeful. Emphasizes creation of competent institutions that actively work for welfare & benefit of the people.
- Pillars of Constitutional Governance
	- Uphold rule of law - everybody subject to law
	- Separation of power 
	- Checks & Balances - restraint others
	- Protection of Individual rights
	- Democratic principles - doctrine of popular sovereignty, regular fair elections
	- Limited government

### Implementation of Constitutionalism
- Written constitution - as a legal foundation for constitutionalism, codify fundamental principles & laws of the state
- Judicial Review - Independent judiciary interprets & enforces. 
- Constitutional culture - recognition of its authority
- Civil Society participation - hold government accountable

### Conclusion
As the SC said in SR Chaudhary v State of Punjab 2001,
Constitutionalism is not guaranteed by sheer existence of the constitution, what matters are the people's political traditions, their spirit, & their commitment to using the form of political organization they have chosen to secure the preservation of the constitution
