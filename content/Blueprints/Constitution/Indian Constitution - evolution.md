### Constitution
 A constitution is the fundamental law of the land, a binding contract between the government & the governed, establishes the structure of the government & protects individual liberty of the citizens
 
### Significance
- Establishment & Stability of Democracy - helped maintaining a functioning democratic system since 1950, which is rare for post colonial nations
- Protection of Fundamental Rights - safeguard individual freedoms against state encroachment
- Mechanism of expansion & adaptation of rights
- Social Justice & affirmative actions - reservations
- Vision of a welfare state - DPSPs
- Strengthened Local Governance - [[73rd CAA 1992]] [[74th CAA 1992]] constitutionalized PRIs & ULBs, with 1/3rd reservation for women

### Challenges
- Erosion of democratic values - Dissent labelled as anti-national & ED/CBI used as tools of political pressure
- Disregard for individual liberties - Weaponization of laws like [[Unlawful Activities Prevention Act]] 
- Judicial Backlog & Delayed Justice - Over 5 crore pending cases
- Communalism, Erosion of Secular principles - Riots - Mumbai 1992, Gujarat 2002, Delhi 2020
- Decline in Press Freedom - According to World Press Freedom Index, India ranks 151/180
- Decline in Parliamentary productivity - parliament met averaging 70 days/year in past decade, compared to 140 days in 1950s, British Parliament sits for 150 days, American congress 260d
- Misuse of Emergency Powers

### Conclusion
Dr. Ambedkar viewed Constitution as a safeguard against State overreach & majoritarianism
Constitution must restrain state authority & protect individual freedoms. 
We must envisage democracy beyond electoral cycles, ensure accountability, free speech & adherence to constitutional ethos
India's constitution will endure as long as its principles are upheld by its citizens, ensuring that the Constitution remains alive in their actions & aspirations
