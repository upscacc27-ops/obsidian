### Introduction
Constitutional morality refers to adherence not merely to the text of the Constitution, but its values, spirit & underlying principles - such as liberty, equality, fraternity, secularism, rule of law & dignity

### Related Supreme Court judgements
- Naz foundation v NCT of Delhi, 2010 - Upheld constitutional principles over social morality by recognizing dignity & equality in same-sex relationships
- Manoj Narula v UOI, 2014 - SC defined Constitutional morality as adherence to rule of law  avoidance of arbitrary governance
- [[2018 Navtej Singh Johar v UOI]]
- [[2017 Justice KS Puttaswamy v UOI]]

### Conclusion
CM is the animating spirit that enable the Constitution to work beyond its text, especially in situations where discretion, conflict, or silence exists.
As repeatedly affirmed by SC, CM ensures that governance is guided not by transient public opinion or administrative convenience, but by enduring constitutional values
