### Introduction
Preamble is the introductory statement to our constitution that embodies its fundamental philosophy, ideals & objectives

### Roots
JL Nehru's Objectives Resolution of December 13, 1946. Adopted by Constituent Assembly on January 22, 1947

### Description

|                         |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| We, the people of India | signifies the doctrine of popular sovereignty - Constitution derives its authority of people                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| SOVEREIGN               | India is neither a dominion nor a dependency, there is no authority above it, is free to conduct its affairs, it can acquire or cede a territory.                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| SOCIALIST               | term added by [[42nd CAA 1976]], values already present especially in DPSPs. Indian socialism is different from Communistic Socialism, India has a mixed economy where public & private sectors exist side-by-side                                                                                                                                               |
| SECULAR                 | term added by [[42nd CAA 1976]], values already present, [[Art 25 Freedom of conscience & free profession, practice &   propagation of religion]], Indian Constitution embodies positive secularism- respect all religions                                                                                                                                       |
| DEMOCRATIC              | doctrine of popular sovereignty - govt derives its authority from will of people expressed through Universal Adult Franchise. ### Legal Status of the Preamble

- [[1960 Berubari Union Case - Presidential Reference]] - Preamble is not a part of the constitution
- [[1973 Keshavananda Bharti v UOI]] - Preamble is a part of the constitution, but neither a source of power nor a limitation on govt. authority. still plays a crucial role in interpretations
- 1975 Union of India v LIC - reaffirmed that Preamble is an integral part of the Constitution, not directly enforceable

### Significance & Limitations

| Significance                                                                                                                      | Limitations                                          |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| establishes popular sovereignty                                                                                                   | Not enforceable - not a source of power              |
| embodies fundamental values - justice, equality, liberty, fraternity                                                              | does not override explicit constitutional provisions |
| defines nature of Indian state                                                                                                    | Broad & ambiguous terminology                        |
| sets the direction for policy making - welfare state, affirmative actions, social justice                                         | Amendable in nature                                  |
| interpretive tool, ex - [[1994 S. R. Bommai v UOI]], term **Secular** helped SC declare secularism as part of the basic structure |                                                      |
| Preamble as integral part of the constitution & part of basic structure                                                           |                                                      |

### Conclusion
Preamble reflects the aspirations of the freedom struggle & serves a guiding light for interpretation & governance. However, as SC said, preamble is the key to understand the Constitution, not the constitution itself
