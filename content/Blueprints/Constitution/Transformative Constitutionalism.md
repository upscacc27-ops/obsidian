>[!quote]  Justice Dipak Mishra in [[2018 Navtej Singh Johar v UOI]]
>Purpose of having a constitution is to transform the society for better & this objective is the fundamental pillar of transformative constitutionalism

### Introduction
refers to the idea that Constitution is not merely a legal framework but a tool for deep social transformation
it aims at dismantling historical inequalities, building a more just, egalitarian & democratic society

### Significance of Transformative constitutionalism
- Completes the freedom project, realizing Founders' vision- Political independence did not end caste, gender & economic oppression. Ex - Abolition of untouchability, reservations for the marginalized
- Keeps the Constitution dynamic - evolution. Ex - Expansion of [[Art 21 Right to life and personal liberty]]
- Ensures substantive equality - Striking down discriminatory practices against sexual minorities[[2018 Navtej Singh Johar v UOI]]
- Reforms the personal laws - Brings arbitrary & regressive practices like triple talaq under constitutional scrutiny [[2017 Shayara Bano v UOI]]
- Upholds Constitutional morality - prioritize constitutional values over social customs [[2018 Indian Young Lawyers Association v State of Kerala]]
- Impacted areas
	- **Caste** - [[1992 Indra Sawhney v UOI]]
	- **Gender** - [[1997 Vishaka v State of Rajasthan]], [[2018 Indian Young Lawyers Association v State of Kerala]], [[2018 Joseph Shine v Union of India]]
	- **LGBTQ+ Rights** - [[2018 Navtej Singh Johar v UOI]]
	- **Privacy** - [[2017 Justice KS Puttaswamy v UOI]]
	- **Environment** - [[1986 MC Mehta v UOI]]


### Conclusion
Indian constitution is a transformative & dynamic charter, envisaged by the founding fathers to secure social, economic & political justice in evolving & diverse society.
Transformative constitutionalism enables itself to move beyond formal guarantees & actively address concerns like inequalities & social exclusion.
