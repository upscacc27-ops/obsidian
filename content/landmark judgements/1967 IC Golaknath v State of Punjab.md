### Supreme Court Judgement
- overruled earlier verdicts in [[1951 Shankari Prasad v UOI]] & Sajjan Singh v State of Rajasthan 1967
- held that amending power under Art 368 cannot be used to take away Fundamental Rights under Part III of the constitution
- Constitutional Amendment **is a law** within the meaning of Art 13(2)
- Court ruled that [[1st CAA 1951]], [[4th CAA 1955]] && [[17th CAA 1964]] will remain valid in future - no retrospective operations
- **Impact** - [[24th CAA 1971]] was enacted, that stated that Parliament under Art 368 has the power to amend any part of the constitution including FRs. Constitutional Amendment is not a law under the definition of Art 13(2)
