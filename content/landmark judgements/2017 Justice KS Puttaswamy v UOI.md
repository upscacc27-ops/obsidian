### Supreme Court Judgement
- declared right to privacy as FR - intrinsic under [[Art 21 Right to life and personal liberty]]
- privacy safeguards individual autonomy & recognizes ability of individual to control vital aspects of life
- right to privacy is not an absolute right, subject to reasonable restrictions
- any law that tends to encroach on right to privacy must withstand the test of restrictions being reasonable
- any invasion on privacy must meet 
	1. legality - stipulates existence of a fair, just & reasonable law
	2. need - legitimate state aim
	3. proportionality - rationale between objectives & means adopted
- **Impact** - overruled [[1954 MP Sharma v Satish Chandra]] & [[1962 Kharak Singh v State of UP]],  where SC had ruled that Right to Privacy is not protected by the Constitution, influenced later judgments like [[2018 Young Lawyers Association case]] ,  [[2018 Joseph Shine v Union of India]], [[2018 Navtej Singh Johar v UOI]]
