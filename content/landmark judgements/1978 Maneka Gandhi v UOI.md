### Supreme Court judgement
- overruled earlier judgement in [[1950 AK Gopalan v State of Madras]] 
- declared section 10(3)(c) of Passport Act, 1967 not violative of [[Art 14 Equality before Law & Equal Protection of Laws]], [[Art 19(1)(a) Freedom of Speech or Expression]], [[Art 19(1)(g) Freedom of profession]] and [[Art 21 Right to life and personal liberty]]  but,
	- above articles are not mutually exclusive, i.e. a law related to personal liberty must not be violative of either Art 21, 14 or Art 19 
	- *Personal Liberty* is of widest amplitude, covers a variety of rights 
	- Right to Life includes all those aspects which make a person's life meaningful, complete & worth living - not merely animal existence but dignified life
	- *Procedure established by Law* means the law must be just, fair and reasonable, not arbitrary, fanciful & oppressive & **procedure should confirm to principle of natural justice**
- **Impact**: introduced *due process of law* into Indian judicial interpretation,  firmly established golden triangle of Art 14, Art 19, Art 21, expanded the scope of [[Art 21 Right to life and personal liberty]]
