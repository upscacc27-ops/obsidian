### Supreme Court Judgement
- reaffirmed its judgement delivered in the Waman Rao case, 1980
- held the amendments made after [[1973 Keshavananda Bharti v UOI]] by which the 9th schedules is amended by including various laws therein, would be open to challenge on grounds of violation of the basic structure.
- The fundamental question before the Court was whether after the verdict on [[1973 Keshavananda Bharti v UOI]], Parliament under Art 31B can immunize legislations from FRs by putting them into 9th Schedule. It established that SC is the final arbiter in deciding if any law violates constitutional identity.
