### SC judgement
- Right to education is a fundamental right and directly flows from right to life
- Only children have this right to free education until age of 14, after that obligation of state to provide education depends upon economic capacity & development
- State obliged to follow directions under Art 45 (free & comp edu for 14 years (now 6 after amendment))
- Art 21 to be construed in light of [[Art 41 DPSP Aim to create a welfare state]], [[Art 45 DPSP Early Childhood Care & Education]] and [[Art 46 DPSP Promote Educational interests of marginalized]]
- Above obligations can be discharged by establishing state's own institutions or aiding/affiliating private institutions
- Unaided private institutions running professional courses can charge a higher fee than state institutions, up to a ceiling to prevent commercialization
- **Impact**: 86th CAA inserted [[Art 21A Right to Education]] an independent fundamental right in 2002. Parliament enacted [[2009 Right of children to free and compulsory Education Act]]
