### Supreme Court judgement
- overruled the earlier verdict in [[1967 IC Golaknath v State of Punjab]]
- held that the Parliament has power to amend any part of the Constitution under Art 368, including Fundamental Rights, except the Basic Structure of the Constitution
- Upheld validity of [[24th CAA 1971]] which inserted Art 13(4) & Art 368(3)
- held Section 2(a) & 2(b) of [[25th CAA 1971]] valid, Section 3 - first part as valid, **second as** **invalid**
- Validated [[29th CAA 1971]]
- **Impact** - led to emergence of Basic Structure Doctrine as a limitation to amending power, which has been applied to subsequent cases
