2nd ARC Report Overview

- est. 31 August 2005
- aimed to overhaul India’s public administrative framework to enhance efficiency, transparency, and accountability.
- Operational from 2006 to 2009, it produced 15 detailed reports addressing critical areas like ethics in governance, local administration, crisis management, and e-governance, serving as key references for policymakers and civil service aspirants.

1. Right to Information - Master Key to Good Governance: Emphasized the importance of the Right to Information (RTI) Act in promoting transparency.
2. Recommended measures to strengthen the implementation of RTI.
3. Unlocking Human Capital: Suggested reforms in education and training for civil servants. Proposed a framework for performance-based evaluation.
4. Crisis Management: Recommended establishing a National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA).
5. Focused on improving disaster preparedness and response mechanisms.
6. Ethics in Governance: Proposed a code of ethics for public officials.
7. Suggested mechanisms for accountability and grievance redressal.
8. Public Order: Addressed issues related to law enforcement and public safety.
9. Recommended reforms to enhance police accountability and efficiency.
10. Local Governance: Advocated for strengthening local self-governments (Panchayati Raj Institutions). Suggested measures for enhancing citizen participation in local governance.
11. Capacity Building for Conflict Resolution: Emphasized the need for capacity building in conflict resolution. Recommended establishing frameworks for inter-state cooperation.
12. Combating Terrorism: Suggested a multi-faceted approach to counter-terrorism.
13. Focused on community engagement and intelligence sharing.
14. Social Capital: A Shared Destiny: Highlighted the role of social capital in governance.
15. Recommended fostering community networks for better governance.
16. Refurbishing Personnel Administration & Scaling New Heights: Suggested reforms in recruitment, training, and performance appraisal of civil servants.
17. Proposed the establishment of a National Institute of Public Administration.
18. Promoting E-Governance: Advocated for leveraging technology to improve service delivery.
19. Recommended frameworks for integrating e-governance into administrative processes.
20. Citizen-Centric Administration: Emphasized the need for a citizen-centric approach in governance.
21. Suggested reforms to enhance accessibility and responsiveness of services.
22. Organizational Structure of Government of India: Recommended restructuring government departments for better efficiency.
23. Advocated for flatter organizational structures to reduce bureaucratic delays.
24. Strengthening Financial Management Systems: Proposed reforms to enhance transparency and accountability in public financial management.
25. Suggested performance budgeting as a tool for effective resource allocation.
26. State and District Administration: Focused on improving administrative efficiency at state and district levels.
27. Recommended decentralization of powers to enhance local governance
